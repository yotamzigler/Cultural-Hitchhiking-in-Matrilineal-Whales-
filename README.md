# Cultural-Hitchhiking-in-Matrilineal-Whales-
This study reproduces the findings of Hal Whitehead’s 2005 paper, Genetic Diversity in
Matrilineal Whales: Models of Cultural Hitchhiking and Group‐Specific Non‐Heritable
Demographic Variation. Whitehead proposed the cultural hitchhiking hypothesis to ex‐
plain the unusually low mitochondrial DNA (mtDNA) diversity observed in matrilineal
whales. Using a stochastic model, he demonstrated the evolutionary effects of gene‐
culture coevolution and examined the role of non‐heritable demographic processes. This
paper details the reproduction of Whitehead’s work through Python‐based simulation.
The key patterns of the original study were successfully reproduced, supporting White‐
head’s conclusions while providing new insights and opportunities for model refine‐
ment.
